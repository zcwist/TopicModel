    Week 9 Discussion: Framing and Reframing

    Josh Scanlan
    Mar 9 at 1:57pm
    171 171 unread replies. 171 171 replies.

    This week we launch the exploration of the problem spaces each of your teams is exploring.  You will do a combination of interviewing and online research to explore that space.  The readings for this week set you up to frame and reframe the problem you are tackling by viewing it as a system, and by doing interviews to learn more about it from others.

    As you watch the "Simplifying Complexity" video, read the "Frame and Reframe" article, and skim the materials in "Talking with Humans" and "Note on Observe" think about the system in which your problem sits.  (You may find the additional reading from "Thinking in Systems" helpful as well.)  For this week's discussion:

    • State the "how might we?" question on which your team is focused to start.
    • Describe what you think the core elements of the system that sits around that "how might we?" are
    • Ask two questions of your classmates about things in that system that you don't yet fully understand

    Post your response and help out two of your classmates with their system questions.

                            My group’s “How might we” is “How might we revolutionize travel by encouraging people to explore new cities and their local community with ease?” From the readings, I’ve come to realize that the core elements of the “How might we” question are rooted in examining problems from the outside in. In the Ted Talk on Simplifying Complexity, he explains that by zooming out and embracing complexity, that will help us zoom in on the details that matter most. I really enjoyed Sarah’s article on framing and reframing. Her examples about building a bridge was really key to my understanding of the process of design thinking. Instead of immediately asking the logistics of how we are going to build the bridge, we should ask why this bridge needs to be built and who is using it, and run with that to find solutions that might be completely different from a bridge. I also enjoyed the part about gum and how instead of just looking at the problem of solving bad breath, many other elements need to be considered such as the Chinese culture which makes talking about bad breath a shameful thing, affecting the product’s success and marketability. I was pleasantly surprised to see Charles & Ray Eamses’ video mentioned. I watched that for the first time last semester in my Film R1A and that example of reframing to understand a situation really resonated with me. I love Carl Sagan’s speech titled ‘Pale Blue Dot’ which reframes the Earth and all our human problems by showing an image of the Earth from extremely far where the Earth looks like a smudge. It really reframes our problems and allows us to reconsider how minuscule our problems truly are. My favorite part of the system is definitely the data collection, observation, interviewing and experimentation. I loved reading about Ethnography because people watching and observing how people operate is something I already enjoy. What kind of put me off about the Note on Observe were the intentions of design. The piece mentions that “Finding new ways to fill the gaps in people’s lives is at the heart of innovation – whether of products, services, organizations or entire businesses.” It sounds awfully materialistic and consumeristic to fill gaps in people’s lives with cool and new innovative things. Maybe I have trouble admitting that products do fill gaps in my life but, my first question would be, can innovation exist without people feeling like there are gaps in their lives that need to be filled?Beyond that, I appreciate how the system allows us to first understand the user and asking why and experimenting, by considering outside components to reframe and better understand the single problem at hand. I understand the stories in and the importance of listening to people’s experiences and stories, but I don't quite understand stories out and how that contributes to ideation.

                                                  How might we to retrofit buildings for sustainability creatively while keeping costs low?

                                                   

                                                  CORE ELEMENTS

                                                  Government: City planners can gather economic and environmental impact reports

                                                      My group is called Gym Class Villains and our “How might we?” question includes “How might we make fitness easier and more accessible to the average American?” By answering this question, our team ultimately hopes to simplify fitness for everyone and find an easy way to incorporate it into everyone’s busy schedules.
